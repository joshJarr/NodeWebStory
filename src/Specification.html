Specification For "Dream; the adventure"

Analysis

The initial aim for my project is to create and represent a dream using an artistic and minimalistic design to generate interest and raise awareness of the human brain's ability to generate scenes and environments from simple memories. The reason I want to raise awareness for this strange activity is due to the increasing population of the western world who are under a great deal of stresses and strains and therefore don't tend to experience much of the REM sleep that is required for dreaming. Essentially the artwork is designed to inadvertently raise awareness for the lack of a persons sleep; an interesting concept due to the amount of damage sleep deprivation can do to a person. A lack of sleep can be directly linked to a difficulty concentrating and people making simple mistakes; therefore in my game I wish to create simple challenges that require a person to concentrate and remember routes and paths taken as they progress through the game. I also wish to create a story that can loop forever, helping generate the feel and function of a real dream.

The idea is to create a piece of artwork to be displayed in a gallery, be it physical or online. Therefore to allow for it to be show cross-platform the code and functionality must be designed for and then tested on multiple platforms, the main ones I will be focusing on are the latest updates for Macintosh and Windows as these are the systems most of my audience use. All browsers would also ideally be supported, and I will initially aim to be able to cover the four most popular, Google chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Internet Explorer. The reasoning behind making the game suitable cross platform is because of the audience and ways in which it will be displayed. Audience: My primary audience is anyone with an interest in the subject. A large portion of these people would be academics, researchers, and students similar to myself. This wide audience could make difficult to keep the attention and interest of everyone, therefore I aim to use a strong narrative and art system to keep a viewer playing. K.I.S.S: Keep It Simple Stupid. Keeping my game simple is important. I don't want masses and masses of text as reading it could become monotonous to allot of viewers and seriously disrupt the flow of the game. I would like my viewers to be able to play the game almost in 'autopilot' mode, relying on simple titles and images to provide information and narrative.

Design

The Narrative

The player plays as a 'dream world version' of themselves. The whole game will be in first person, helping generate the feeling of individuality and helping to achieve my aim; to make the user relate to their own sleeping and dreaming pattern. The user will pick out decisions that will lead down certain paths, however the user cannot 'lose' or 'die' as they simple 'wake up' or respawn at the start again, having to remember the decisions of their previous encounter, this is where the concentration and memory aspect come into play, two abilities affected by sleep deprivation.

The game starts from the user 'waking up' in a foreign land, similar to most real life dreams. The player is not directly told they are in a dream, therefore they work it out themselves when playing, also an aspect which is similar to a real life dream. A plain and simple artwork combines with simple narrative with harsh consequences creates an eerie and almost unnatural feel to the play, this is also similar to most dreams, as many people experience a familiarity to the environments in their own dream, probably because of the input of the subconscious. Therefore a familiar environment of a beach has been selected for the dream, and due to the simplistic design allot of the scene is left to the viewers imagination, making the area feel more individual to the player. Obvious and clear areas are used, such as forests, mountains, and deserts, this helps create a further sense of familiarity with the scenes, these clearly stated zones will also help prevent viewers feeling lost in the environment.

the goal of the dream is simply to be woken up by the alarm, however the player is not told this and has to work it out themselves. Every time the player 'dies' in the dream causes them to wake up, and then restart the dream once then fall asleep, meaning the goal is to get to the end with as little interrupted sleep as possible. Waking up while sleeping at night is inevitable, however by concentrating and paying attention to their previous action the player can help prevent themselves from waking up too often in the game and get the best sleep. Restarting back to the beginning will also cause mild to extreme frustration, especially if the player can already made the same mistake before, this frustration in combination with the test of memory and concentration is exactly the opposite feeling and actions of sleeping and dreaming creating the contrast needed to make the viewer to reflect of their own sleeping patterns. A simple narrative of exploring the environment and surviving is used allowing users to concentrate of the pathways they take, however difficulty in the game comes when certain pathways lead back to other pathways. Essentially the game is not linear and there are multiple ways of completing it, however without knowing this the player may spend a long time remembering the longest route around when there is a much shorter route; this helps reward the players for being daring and taking risks. To generate a eerie feel to the game strange creatures such as bears and an unknown entity thats is encounterd but never visually seen, only herd, this will also generate a curiosity of what these creatures are, and this is answerd at the end of the game.

As previously mentioned a simplistic design and minimalistic layout is used to allow the intervention of the players imagination, it also allows the story to be rememberd easily as there is no infomation overload, aiding the compleation. I did this as I wanted the dificulty to come from the similarity of story componants and not similarity of the pages look or feel. The colour scheme is also used to help represent a dream, as it is thought that people dream in black and white. Therefore I have chosen to use shades of brown to represent this, color is only used in exteme conditions, such as when the user fails (to help memory of the area) and at the end of the game as a sort of 'reward'. Fadeing the pages in are also direct reprersenting of a dream, creating a 'soft' feel, similar to the idea of a person drifting through the game as they would through
